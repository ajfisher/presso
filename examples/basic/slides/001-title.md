# Presso

Markdown-native web presentations.

:::notes
Opening notes for the basic fixture.

Welcome everyone and settle into the room before moving too quickly. This opening is intentionally a little longer so the presenter view has enough text to behave like a real teleprompter during rehearsal.

Use this first slide to frame the promise of the deck. Mention that Presso is intended to keep authoring simple, keep Markdown as the source of truth, and make the speaker experience feel calm during delivery.

Pause briefly after the framing line, then point out that the browser runtime, notes, controller, and export paths are all part of the same lightweight workflow. The audience should understand that this is not just a slide renderer, it is the speaking surface as well.

Keep the tone measured. The teleprompter should have enough room to scroll without forcing the presenter to chase the text. The first paragraph gives the initial pacing delay, while the remaining paragraphs prove that the scroll speed can be tuned.

Use a calm pace here and watch the notes move only after the opening thought has had time to land. If the speaker speeds up, the faster control should make the movement feel more assertive without changing slides or affecting the timer.

If the speaker pauses, the pause control should freeze the notes exactly where they are. That matters in real conference delivery because interruptions, audience questions, and demo transitions often happen while the speaker is still on the same slide.

The reset control should return the notes to the top of this slide. That is useful during rehearsal, but it is also useful if a slide gets revisited during a question or when the speaker decides to repeat the opening setup.

These fixture notes deliberately run longer than a normal title slide. The goal is not to write the perfect talk, it is to ensure the runtime has enough content to prove scrolling behavior in a browser test.

Close this opening by moving naturally into the vertical slice slide. Do not rush the transition; the point of the example deck is to make the presenter tools visible without becoming the subject of the talk.
:::
